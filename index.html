Rutherford and Geiger experiment

Why did Rutherford perform the experiment and what were his expectations?

Before Rutherford and Geiger performed their experiment, they made their predictions based on
Thomson’splum-pudding model. The model suggested the atom is like a positively charged pudding
in which the electrons move around freely within that region like the plums in the pudding.
This model suggested that the atom’s mass is equally distributed.

Rutherford’s and Geiger’s experiment involved the emission of alpha particles towards a thin
gold foil. They reasoned that if Thomson’s model is correct then there will be little to no
reflection of the alpha particles since the mass is equally distributed.They expected all of
the alpha particles to pass through the gold foil.

Experiment model

They used a radioactive element, which naturally decays, to emit alpha particles.
They used Radium and enclosed it within a container and left a tiny hole to let the
beams out in one direction. The beam was directed towards a gold foil that was surrounded
by detectors that were coated with fluorescent substances.

What they discovered

They discovered that most of the alpha particles passed through the gold foil and only some got completely
reflected and others were deflected at a large angle. They concluded that Thomson’s model is wrong and
that the atom is mostly empty space. Additionally, based on the law of magnetic reaction they concluded that
the alpha particles are being deflected by a small positively charged particle in the center of the atom;
they called it the proton. They proposed a new a model of the atom in which the proton(nucleus) is in the
center of a mostly empty space atom that is surrounded by electrons.
